Collaborative Secure Federated Learning example workflow

In this example, we train collaboratively two mortality classification models (logistic regression and Cox regression) on the dataset D2D2, split across three nodes. We use a Secure Federated Learning workflow supported by Tune Insight's platform, where models are trained locally using Pytorch library and are aggregated across parties under homomorphic encryption. Local models are never revealed to the other parties, and only the collective global model is shared across them.

Prepare results

We evaluate the training of a Cox regression and a Logistic regression on the mortality dataset D2D2, and compare three scenarios to differentiate the impact of distribution and encryption on the obtained result:

  • Local: local training of the corresponding model, using all the data (ideal case where data can be centralized, and the whole process can be run with no encryption or additional protection mechanism)
  • Federated (Federated Learning): the raw data is not shared across the different parties; only the local models are shared across all parties, and are aggregated in the clear. The contribution form each party is disclosed to the others, and the iterative process is vulnerable to inference and reidentification attacks
  • Secure Federated: Neither the raw data nor the local models are shared in the clear. The locally-trained models are encrypted and aggregated under encryption, and differentially-private noise is added to the result before it is released and decrypted to feed the next iteration of training. Local contributions to the global model are not disclosed, and inference and reidentification attacks are mitigated.

display_tables = {}
reference_node = 1
for metric in ['auroc', 'loss']:
    display_tables[metric] = {}
    for mode, mode_item in final_results.items():
        display_tables[metric][mode] = {}
        for reg_type, reg_type_item in mode_item.items():
            if mode == 'Local':
                display_tables[metric][mode][reg_type] = reg_type_item[metric]
            else:
                display_tables[metric][mode][reg_type] = reg_type_item[reference_node][metric]
